MintMD Privacy Policy

Mint Venture Partners (hereinafter referred to as "Company") ensures that all personal information it handles is thoroughly protected in compliance with domestic privacy laws, including the Personal Information Protection Act and the Act on Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection (hereinafter referred to as the "Information and Communications Network Act"). This privacy policy aims to protect users' rights and interests and to facilitate the resolution of any user complaints regarding personal information.


Article 1 (Purpose of Processing Personal Information)

The Company processes personal information for the following purposes. The personal information is not used for purposes other than those listed below, and if the purpose of use changes, the Company will take necessary actions, such as obtaining separate consent in accordance with Article 18 of the Personal Information Protection Act.

  1. Membership Registration and Management

    • Confirm the intent to join the expert network.

    • Confirm the intent to join and manage advisory services.

    • Confirm the intent to join and manage employment services.

    • Confirm the intent to join and manage recruitment services.

  2. Service Provision and Development of New Services

    • Fulfill contracts related to the provision of advisory and consulting services.

    • Fulfill contracts related to the provision of employment and recruitment services.

    • Communication related to services.

    • Payment management for compensating experts.

    • Fee management for recruitment services.

  3. Information Provision and Complaint Handling

    • Verify identity of complainants and service customers.

    • Notify or contact individuals for fact-finding or result notification.

    • Provide information related to events and participation opportunities.

    • Provide advertising and marketing information.

Article 2 (Processing and Retention Period of Personal Information)

The Company processes and retains personal information within the retention period specified by law or within the retention period agreed upon by the data subject at the time of information

collection.

  • Membership Registration and Management Personal information is retained until the member withdraws from the expert network, advisory services, employment services, or recruitment services. However, if the following circumstances apply, the information is retained until the reason for retention is resolved:

    • If an investigation is ongoing due to a violation of relevant laws, information is retained until the investigation is completed.

    • If there are outstanding debts arising from service use, information is retained until the debt is settled.

  • Service Provision Personal information is retained until service provision is completed and payment is fully settled. However, if the following circumstances apply, the information is retained until the specified period ends:

    • Records on displays and advertisements related to transactions (according to the "Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc."):

      • Records on display and advertisement: 6 months

      • Records on contracts or subscription withdrawal, payment, and supply of goods: 5 years

      • Records on consumer complaints or dispute resolution: 3 years


Article 3 (Personal Information Items Processed)

The Company processes the following personal information:

  1. Membership Registration and Management

    • For individuals joining the expert network

      (Required) Identity verification: Name, affiliated institution, user ID, and password

      (Optional) Home address, home phone number, work address, work phone number, past advisory and consulting experience, major field, certification/license number, areas of interest

    • For companies and institutions joining the advisory service

      (Required) Representative's name, contact person’s name, contact person's mobile number, and email address

  2. Service Provision and New Service Development

    • For individuals joining the expert network

      (Required) Education, career, qualifications, profile information, resume, job history, related qualifications, bank account information, bank name, account holder's name, social security number

      (Optional) Past advisory and consulting experience, major field, certification/license number, areas of interest

  3. Information Provision and Complaint Handling (Required) Membership registration information and contact details


Article 4 (Provision of Personal Information to Third Parties)

The Company processes personal information within the scope of the purposes specified in this privacy policy and only provides personal information to third parties when permitted by law or with the consent of the data subject.

Personal information of experts and individuals utilizing MintMD’s advisory and recruitment services may be provided to the opposing party to facilitate smooth service delivery. In these

cases, the information will only be shared within the necessary scope with the consent of the data subject.


Article 5 (Entrustment of Personal Information Processing)

The Company entrusts the processing of personal information to third parties to facilitate efficient management, as outlined below:

  • Website operation and maintenance: Amongsotfware Co., Ltd. (until the contract ends or membership is withdrawn)

  • Identity verification: NICE Information Service Co., Ltd. (until the contract ends or membership is withdrawn)


Article 6 (Destruction of Personal Information)

The Company will destroy personal information without delay when it becomes unnecessary due to the expiration of the retention period or the achievement of the processing purpose. If certain

personal information must be retained for legal reasons, it is stored separately in a different database or location.

The procedures and methods for destruction are as follows:

  1. Destruction Procedures: The Company selects the personal information that needs to be destroyed and receives approval from the privacy officer before destruction.

  2. Destruction Methods:

    • Electronic files are permanently deleted using methods that prevent recovery.

    • Paper records are shredded or incinerated.


Article 7 (Rights of Data Subjects and Legal Representatives)

Data subjects can exercise their rights to request access, correction, deletion, and suspension of the processing of their personal information at any time. These rights can be exercised through written, electronic, or fax communication, and the Company will promptly act upon such

requests.

Data subjects can exercise their rights through an agent, such as a legal representative or a

delegated person. In this case, a power of attorney must be submitted following the relevant legal form.


Article 8 (Measures to Ensure the Security of Personal Information)

The Company takes the following measures to ensure the security of personal information:

  • Managerial Measures: Establish and implement internal management plans and provide regular education to employees handling personal information.

  • Technical Measures:

    • Restrict access to personal information by granting, changing, or revoking access rights to the database system.

    • Store and manage user information in encrypted form.

    • Use security programs to prevent personal information leaks or damage caused by hacking, viruses, etc.

  • Physical Measures: Control access to the computer room, data storage room, and other physical locations where personal information is stored.
